# Dashboard Overview

The **Dashboard** is the central control panel of the system. It provides a real-time summary of business performance across Sales, Purchases, Inventory, and Finance modules in a single view.

## Who Can Access

Access to dashboard data depends on user role and permissions:

- **Administrators**: Full access to all branches, reports, and system-wide metrics.
- **Store Managers**: Access limited to assigned shop or warehouse data.
- **Sales Managers**: Sales performance, targets, and invoice history.
- **Accountants / Finance Officers**: Financial summaries, expenses, and payable/receivable tracking.

---

## Navigation

- **Menu Path**: Dashboard
- **Default Page**: Automatically loads after successful login

---

## Dashboard Sections

The dashboard is divided into four main functional sections:

---

### 1. Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

At the top of the dashboard, KPI cards provide quick business insights:

- **Today's Sales**: Total confirmed sales revenue for the current day.
- **Today's Purchases**: Total purchase cost recorded today.
- **Today's Expenses**: Operational and administrative expenses.
- **Total Stock Value**: Estimated value of current inventory (based on cost price).
- **Active Receivables / Payables**: Outstanding customer dues and supplier liabilities.

---

### 2. Analytical Charts

Visual analytics to understand business trends:

- **Monthly Sales vs Purchases**: Compares revenue and procurement over time.
- **Expense Distribution Chart**: Breakdown of expenses by category.

---

### 3. Recent Activity & Alerts

Real-time operational updates:

- **Recent Sales**: Latest generated invoices (last 5–10 records).
- **Pending Approvals**: Documents awaiting authorization (e.g., Purchase Orders).
- **Low Stock Alerts**: Products below minimum stock threshold.

---

## KPI Reference Table

| KPI           | Calculation                   | Purpose                         |
| ------------- | ----------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Sales Card    | Invoice total after discounts | Measures revenue performance    |
| Purchase Card | Total incoming stock cost     | Tracks procurement spending     |
| Stock Value   | Quantity × average cost       | Represents inventory investment |
| Expense Card  | All operational expenses      | Monitors business cost control  |
| Low Stock     | Stock ≤ minimum level         | Triggers replenishment action   |

## Step-by-Step Guide

### View Shop-Specific Dashboard Data

1. Locate the **Shop Filter** in the top toolbar.
2. Select the desired **Shop / Warehouse**.
3. Dashboard updates automatically with filtered data.

---

### Approve Pending Documents

1. Scroll to **Pending Approvals** section.
2. Click on a document ID (e.g., `PO-1029`).
3. Review details in the popup or details page.
4. Click **Approve** or **Reject**.

---

## Business Rules

- **Data Access Control**: Users only see data based on assigned roles and branch permissions.
- **Real-Time Updates**: Sales and stock changes reflect instantly across dashboard widgets.
- **Low Stock Logic**: Alerts trigger when stock reaches or falls below minimum stock level defined in product configuration.

---

## Business Example

A Store Manager logs in at 10:00 AM and sees:

- **Today's Sales**: $1,250 → indicates healthy morning activity.
- **Low Stock Alert**: "A4 Copy Paper – 5 reams remaining"

They check inventory, confirm 50 reams are available in the warehouse, and immediately initiate a **Stock Requisition** to replenish the shop.
