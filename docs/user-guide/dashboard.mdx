# Dashboard Overview

The **Dashboard** is the command center of your system. It aggregates real-time data from all modules—Sales, Purchases, Inventory, and Finance—and presents them in intuitive visual charts and key metrics.

## Who Can Access

* **Administrators**: Full access to all business metrics and locations.
* **Store Managers**: Metrics scoped to their specific assigned shop or warehouse.
* **Sales Managers**: Sales-specific metrics, targets, and history.
* **Accountants / Finance Officers**: Financial balances, expense widgets, and loan details.

---

## Navigation

* **Menu Path**: Dashboard (Topmost item on the Sidebar)
* **Default landing page**: Displays immediately after logging in.

## Screen Overview

The Dashboard is broken down into four main sections:

### 1. Key Performance Indicator (KPI) Cards
Located at the top of the page, these summaries show daily, weekly, or monthly figures:
* **Today's Sales**: Total invoice amounts settled today.
* **Today's Purchases**: Total stock purchase bills logged today.
* **Today's Expenses**: Operational costs entered.
* **Total Stock Value**: Estimated financial value of items currently held in warehouses and shops.
* **Active Receivables/Payables**: Dues outstanding from customers and dues owed to suppliers.

### 2. Analytical Charts
Interactive charts showing trends over time:
* **Monthly Sales & Purchases Comparison**: Line/bar chart representing seasonal trends.
* **Expense Breakdown**: Donut chart showing expense distribution by category.

### 3. Recent Transactions & Activity Logs
* **Recent Sales List**: The latest 5 or 10 invoices generated.
* **Pending Approvals**: Quick links to documents requiring your verification (e.g. Purchase Orders or Stock Transfers).
* **Low Stock Warning Alert List**: Real-time listing of products whose quantities have fallen below their safety threshold.

## Section Descriptions

| Section | Key Metric / Detail | Business Purpose |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sales Card** | Invoice total minus discounts. | Tracks revenue targets. |
| **Purchase Card** | Total incoming stock bills. | Tracks procurement costs. |
| **Stock Value Card** | Inventory qty × average cost. | Measures capital tied up in stock. |
| **Expense Card** | Petty cash & bank expenses. | Tracks operational overheads. |
| **Low Stock Panel** | Lists product SKU, warehouse, and quantity. | Triggers reorder processes immediately. |

## Step-by-Step Instructions

### Checking Shop-Specific Metrics:
1. Locate the shop filter dropdown on the top toolbar (available for multi-shop administrators).
2. Select your target **Shop** or **Warehouse**.
3. The dashboard will instantly reload and display metrics for the selected location only.

### Approving an Item directly from the Dashboard:
1. Scroll down to the **Pending Approvals** list.
2. Click on the document ID (e.g. PO-1029).
3. The system will open the details view where you can verify the entries and click **Approve** or **Reject**.

---

## Business Rules & Logic

* **Data Scope**: Standard staff members can only view metrics related to their assigned shop branch. Central managers and administrators can view consolidated multi-branch data.
* **Real-time Sync**: POS sales update dashboard cards instantly upon transaction completion. Warehouse physical counts adjust the stock value cards in real time.
* **Low Stock Alerts**: Triggered only when a product's stock count is less than or equal to the "Min Stock Level" defined in the Product Catalog.

## Business Example

A Store Manager logs in at 10:00 AM. They look at the **Key Performance Indicators** and see:
* **Today's Sales**: $1,250 (indicates steady morning traffic).
* **Low Stock Alert**: "Copy Paper A4 - 5 Reams remaining" (reaches threshold).
The manager immediately clicks on the **Low Stock Alert**, sees that the central warehouse has 50 reams, and creates a **Sales Requisition** to restock.
