# Inventory & Catalog Configuration

This guide details product definitions, variations, location management, stock transfers, physical audits, and internal usage.

---

## Who Can Access

* **Product Catalog & Attributes**: Procurement Staff, Product Managers, Admins.
* **Barcode Generator**: Warehouse staff, cashiers.
* **Warehouse setup, Stock transfers, adjustments**: Warehouse supervisors, Shop managers.

---

## 1. Product Catalog & Attribute Configurations

### Overview
Define the core items sold or purchased. The catalog supports rich attribute details:
* **Product List**: CRUD operations for products and their variations.
* **Product Exchange**: Configure product exchange rules.
* **Attributes**: Setup categories, units, sizes, brands, product types, colors, shelves, models, and origins.

### Navigation
* **Product List**: Product Configuration → **Product List**
* **Attributes**: Product Configuration → [Select Attribute, e.g. Product Categories, Sizes, Brands]

### Field Descriptions (Product Setup)

| Field | Description | Rules / Validations |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Product Name** | Clear name of the item. | Mandatory |
| **Category** | Primary department. | Select from pre-setup Categories. |
| **SKU Code** | Unique stock keeping unit. | System generates automatically if left blank. |
| **Measurement Unit**| Unit of sale (Pcs, Box, Ream). | Select from Units setup. |
| **Attributes** | Variation combinations (Color, Size). | Creates unique stock nodes. |
| **Alert Qty** | Safety stock threshold. | Triggers low stock alert on Dashboard. |

### Step-by-Step Instructions (Add Product):
1. Navigate to Product Configuration → **Product List** and click **Add Product**.
2. Enter the Product Name, select its Category, Brand, Origin, Model, and Unit.
3. If the product has attributes (e.g. Size: Medium, Color: Blue), toggle **Has Attributes** and check the relevant combinations.
4. Input the Purchase Cost and Selling Price.
5. Set the safety stock **Alert Quantity**.
6. Click **Save Product**.

## 2. Barcode Generator

### Overview
Generate and print barcode labels for products or specific incoming purchase shipments.

### Navigation
* **Sidebar**: Product Configuration → **Barcode Generator**

### Step-by-Step Instructions:
1. Navigate to **Barcode Generator**.
2. Search and select products.
3. Choose the print format (e.g. 24-sticker sheet or continuous roll).
4. Select the print elements (Show Price, Show Name, Show Brand).
5. Click **Generate Barcode** to download a print-ready PDF.

## 3. Warehouse Setup & Stock Transfers

### Overview
* **Warehouse/Shop Setup**: Establish storage depots and retail shops.
* **Transfer Stock**: Dispatch inventory from one warehouse/shop to another.
* **Requisition Received**: View and ship items requested by retail shops.
* **Shipment Products**: Track dispatches currently in transit.

### Navigation
* **Transfer Stock**: Inventory Management → **Transfer Stock**
* **Requisition Received**: Inventory Management → **Requisition Received**

[Insert Screenshot Here - Stock Transfer Dispatch Screen]

### Step-by-Step Instructions (Warehouse Stock Transfer):
1. Go to **Transfer Stock** and click **New Transfer**.
2. Choose the source warehouse and destination location.
3. Add products, select quantities, and click **Submit Dispatch**.
4. The destination location goes to **Requisition Received**, selects the incoming transfer shipment, verifies the quantities, and clicks **Accept** to increase their local stock.

---

## 4. Stock Adjustments & Damage Entries

### Overview
* **Stock Adjustment**: Manually correct inventory count mismatches following audits.
* **Damage Product Entry**: Write off broken, expired, or spoiled merchandise.
* **Used Product**: Log inventory items pulled for internal office or showroom usage.

### Step-by-Step Instructions (Log Damage Entry):
1. Go to Inventory Management → **Damage Product Entry** and click **Create Damage Log**.
2. Select the **Warehouse** where the damaged items are stored.
3. Add the product row, quantity, and choose the damage reason (e.g. Broken, Expired).
4. Click **Save**. The stock balance is adjusted, and the loss is logged.

---

## Business Rules

* **Attribute Merging**: For products with multiple sizes or colors, the POS screen groups them under a single card. Cashiers select the specific combination in a popup modal.
* **Safety Threshold Alerts**: Low stock warnings appear on the dashboard only if the available quantity is less than or equal to the defined **Alert Qty**.
