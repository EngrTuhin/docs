# Purchase Management

Purchase Management is the core module used to handle all product purchasing activities in the system.

It helps businesses control stock inflow, manage suppliers, and track purchase-related transactions from order to delivery.

---

# Purpose of Purchase Management

This module allows you to:

- Create purchase orders for suppliers
- Track incoming stock
- Manage supplier-wise purchasing history
- Handle purchase invoices
- Process purchase returns
- Maintain accurate inventory levels
- Control procurement workflow

---

# Purchase Workflow Overview

A typical purchase flow is:

1. Create Purchase Order
2. Send Order to Supplier
3. Receive Products (Purchase Invoice)
4. Update Stock Automatically
5. Handle Purchase Returns (if needed)
6. Track All Purchases in Reports

---

# Purchase Management Modules

| Module                | Purpose                               |
| --------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Create Purchase Order | Create and manage supplier orders     |
| Purchase Invoice      | Record received goods and stock entry |
| Purchase Return       | Return goods to supplier              |
| All Purchase Records  | View purchase history                 |
| Supplier Payments     | Manage supplier due payments          |

---

# 1. Create Purchase Order

Create Purchase Order is used to generate and manage purchase requests sent to suppliers.

---

## Purpose

This module is used for:

- Ordering products from suppliers
- Managing low stock items
- Planning inventory replenishment
- Tracking pending supplier orders

---

## How It Works

Purchase Orders are the first step in the procurement process. They do not affect stock immediately but prepare the system for incoming goods.

---

## Purchase Order Process

### Step 1: Select Supplier

Choose the supplier you want to order products from.

---

### Step 2: Create Purchase Order

System generates a PO number automatically (e.g., POA-0001-2026).

---

### Step 3: Add Products

You can add products in two ways:

- Search product manually (by name, SKU, barcode)
- Use quick selection tools:
  - Low Stock Products
  - Out of Stock Products

---

### Step 4: Enter Quantity

Specify how many units you want to order for each product.

---

### Step 5: Save Purchase Order

Once saved, the order is stored with **Pending status**.

---

## Key Features

### Low Stock Shortcut

Quickly add products that are below stock alert level.

### Out of Stock Shortcut

Instantly add products that are completely out of stock.

### Product Search

Search products using:

- Product Name
- SKU
- Barcode

---

## Business Rules

- Purchase Order does NOT increase stock
- Only supplier confirmation is recorded at this stage
- Stock is updated only after Purchase Invoice
- Each PO is linked to a supplier
- PO can remain in Pending, Approved, or Completed state

---

## Purchase Order Lifecycle

| Status             | Meaning                         |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------- |
| Pending            | Order created but not processed |
| Approved           | Supplier accepted the order     |
| Partially Received | Some items delivered            |
| Completed          | Full order received             |
| Cancelled          | Order canceled                  |

---

## Example Use Case

A shop notices low stock of:

- Paracetamol
- Shampoo
- Rice packets

They:

1. Open Purchase Order
2. Click “Low Stock”
3. Select products
4. Add quantities
5. Choose supplier
6. Save PO

System generates a PO ready to send to supplier.

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake                   | Problem               |
| ------------------------- | --------------------- |
| Adding wrong supplier     | Delivery confusion    |
| Not checking low stock    | Overstock or stockout |
| Wrong quantity entry      | Inventory mismatch    |
| Confusing PO with Invoice | Stock not updated     |

---

## Best Practice

- Always use Low Stock feature for efficiency
- Double-check supplier before saving
- Keep PO records organized
- Convert PO to invoice properly

# 2. Purchase Invoice

Purchase Invoice is used to record goods received from suppliers and update stock in the system.

It is the second step in the purchase workflow and is created when physical goods are received from suppliers against a Purchase Order or direct purchase.

---

## Purpose

This module is used for:

- Recording received goods from suppliers
- Increasing warehouse stock automatically
- Tracking supplier payable amounts
- Completing purchase orders
- Maintaining purchase history and cost records

---

## How It Works

Purchase Invoice confirms that goods ordered from suppliers have been delivered and received by the business.

Unlike Purchase Order, this module directly affects stock and accounting.

---

## Purchase Invoice Process

### Step 1: Select Supplier

Choose the supplier from whom goods are received.

---

### Step 2: Fill Basic Information

Enter:

- Purchase Date
- Warehouse / Shop (Required)
- Purchase Order Reference (Optional)

If a Purchase Order is selected, system can auto-load products.

---

### Step 3: Add Products

Products can be added using:

- Product search (Name / Barcode / SKU)
- Selecting from linked Purchase Order

Each product includes:

- Quantity
- Unit Price
- Total Price

---

### Step 4: Apply Discount & VAT (Optional)

You can apply:

- Global Discount (Fixed or Percentage)
- VAT / Tax Calculation

System automatically updates totals.

---

### Step 5: Save Purchase Invoice

After saving:

- Stock is increased in selected warehouse
- Supplier payable amount is recorded
- Invoice is stored in Purchase records

---

## Key Features

### Purchase Order Integration

If linked with PO:

- Products are auto-filled
- Reduces manual work
- Ensures order accuracy

---

### Warehouse Control

Stock is added to selected warehouse only.

Each warehouse maintains separate stock records.

---

### Product Search

Supports search by:

- Product Name
- Barcode
- SKU

---

## Business Rules

- Purchase Invoice increases stock immediately after saving
- Supplier due is created or updated
- Warehouse selection is mandatory
- Purchase Order linkage is optional but recommended
- Invoice cannot exist without supplier

---

## Invoice Status

| Status    | Meaning                           |
| --------- | --------------------------------- |
| Pending   | Invoice created but not finalized |
| Complete  | Stock updated successfully        |
| Partial   | Partially received items          |
| Cancelled | Invoice is voided                 |

---

## Example Use Case

A supplier delivers:

- 20 Paracetamol
- 10 Shampoo bottles
- 5 Boxes of rice

Steps:

1. Open Purchase Invoice
2. Select Supplier
3. Choose Warehouse
4. Add products with quantity
5. Apply discount if needed
6. Save invoice

Result:

- Stock increases automatically
- Supplier balance is updated
- Invoice is marked Complete

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake                   | Problem                      |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------- |
| Wrong warehouse selection | Stock goes to wrong location |
| Incorrect quantity        | Stock mismatch               |
| Missing supplier          | Invoice cannot be tracked    |
| Not linking PO            | Manual errors increase       |

---

## Best Practice

- Always verify supplier before saving
- Use Purchase Order when available
- Double-check quantities before saving
- Use barcode scanning for speed and accuracy
- Keep warehouse selection consistent

# 3. Purchase Return

Purchase Return is used to manage goods returned from the business back to suppliers.

It is used when purchased products are damaged, expired, incorrect, or not needed in stock.

---

## Purpose

This module is used for:

- Returning defective or expired products to suppliers
- Adjusting inventory after return
- Claiming refunds or credit from suppliers
- Tracking purchase return history
- Maintaining accurate stock records

---

## How It Works

Purchase Return is always linked to a Purchase Invoice to ensure correct tracking of returned items.

It reduces stock and updates supplier payable/credit balance.

---

## Purchase Return Process

### Step 1: Select Supplier

Choose the supplier to whom the product is being returned.

---

### Step 2: Select Purchase Invoice

Select the original Purchase Invoice.

This ensures:

- Correct product tracking
- Accurate stock adjustment
- Proper financial record linkage

---

### Step 3: Select Product

Choose the specific product from the invoice that is being returned.

---

### Step 4: Add Return Details

Enter:

- Return Quantity
- Return Reason (optional but recommended)
- Any applicable adjustment or discount

---

### Step 5: Save Purchase Return

After saving:

- Stock is reduced from warehouse
- Return record is stored
- Supplier balance may be adjusted

---

## Purchase Return List

The listing page shows all purchase returns in the system.

---

## Key Information Displayed

Each record contains:

- Return Reference Number
- Return Date
- Supplier Name
- Purchase Invoice Reference
- Warehouse
- Total Return Amount
- Status (if applicable)
- Action Buttons (View, Edit, Print, Delete)

---

## Summary Section

At the top, the system may show:

- Total Returns
- Total Return Amount
- Today’s Returns
- Monthly Return Summary

---

## Business Rules

- Purchase Return must be linked to a Purchase Invoice
- Stock is reduced after return is saved
- Supplier credit or refund is recorded
- Cannot return more than purchased quantity
- Each return is permanently stored in system

---

## Example Use Case

A supplier sends:

- Expired medicine
- Damaged packaging products

Steps:

1. Open Purchase Return List
2. Click Add
3. Select Supplier
4. Select Purchase Invoice
5. Choose product
6. Enter quantity to return
7. Save return

Result:

- Stock decreases automatically
- Return record is created
- Supplier balance is adjusted

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake                      | Problem                    |
| ---------------------------- | -------------------------- |
| Wrong invoice selection      | Incorrect stock adjustment |
| Returning more than received | System validation error    |
| Missing supplier selection   | Return cannot be processed |
| Not specifying product       | Incomplete record          |

---

## Best Practice

- Always verify invoice before return
- Return only linked purchased items
- Add clear return reason
- Keep return records consistent with stock logs

# 4. Direct Purchase Return

Direct Purchase Return is used to return products to suppliers without linking them to a specific Purchase Invoice.

It is a flexible return system used for quick or informal return processing.

---

## Purpose

This module is used for:

- Returning goods without invoice reference
- Handling cash or informal purchases
- Returning damaged or defective products quickly
- Adjusting inventory without invoice dependency
- Recording supplier credit or refund cases

---

## How It Works

Unlike standard Purchase Return, this module does not require a Purchase Invoice.

It directly reduces stock and records a supplier return transaction.

---

## Direct Purchase Return Process

### Step 1: Select Supplier

Choose the supplier to whom the products are being returned.

---

### Step 2: Add Products

Use the product search bar to add items:

- Search by Product Name
- Barcode
- SKU

---

### Step 3: Enter Return Details

For each product, enter:

- Return Quantity
- Unit Price
- Return Reason (if available)
- Any adjustments or remarks

---

### Step 4: Apply Discount (Optional)

You can apply:

- Flat discount
- Percentage discount

System recalculates totals automatically.

---

### Step 5: Save Return

After saving:

- Stock is reduced from warehouse
- Return record is created
- Supplier credit/refund is recorded

---

## Direct Purchase Return List

The listing page shows all direct returns.

---

## Key Information Displayed

Each record includes:

- Reference Number
- Return Date
- Supplier Name
- Warehouse (if applicable)
- Total Quantity Returned
- Total Amount
- Action Buttons (View, Edit, Print, Delete)

---

## Summary Section

The top section may show:

- Total Direct Returns
- Total Return Amount
- Today’s Returns
- Monthly Return Summary

---

## Business Rules

- No Purchase Invoice is required
- Stock is updated immediately after saving
- Supplier balance may be adjusted
- Each return is recorded permanently
- Cannot exceed available stock quantity

---

## Example Use Case

A shop purchases goods informally and later finds:

- Damaged products
- Expired items
- Wrong delivered items

Steps:

1. Open Direct Purchase Return
2. Select Supplier
3. Search and add products
4. Enter quantity and price
5. Save return

Result:

- Stock decreases immediately
- Return record is created
- Supplier credit is recorded

---

## Key Difference from Purchase Return

| Feature          | Purchase Return | Direct Purchase Return   |
| ---------------- | --------------- | ------------------------ |
| Invoice Required | Yes             | No                       |
| Flexibility      | Limited         | High                     |
| Stock Update     | Yes             | Yes                      |
| Use Case         | Formal returns  | Quick / informal returns |

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake                       | Problem              |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------- |
| Wrong supplier selection      | Incorrect accounting |
| Returning more than stock     | Validation error     |
| Not checking price            | Financial mismatch   |
| Confusing with invoice return | Tracking errors      |

---

## Best Practice

- Use Direct Return only when invoice is not available
- Always verify supplier before saving
- Keep reason notes for tracking
- Avoid mixing with formal purchase returns

# 5. Supplier Payment Records

Supplier Payment Records is used to manage all payments made to suppliers against purchase invoices or outstanding dues.

It ensures proper tracking of accounts payable and supplier transaction history.

---

## Purpose

This module is used for:

- Recording payments made to suppliers
- Tracking supplier-wise payment history
- Clearing purchase invoice dues
- Managing cash, bank, or mobile payments
- Maintaining accurate accounting records

---

## How It Works

Supplier payments are created when the business pays money to suppliers for purchased goods.

These payments reduce supplier outstanding balances and update financial records.

---

## Supplier Payment List

The listing page shows all supplier payment transactions.

---

## Key Information Displayed

Each record contains:

- Serial Number (SL)
- Payment Date
- Receipt Number
- Supplier Name (Company)
- Transaction Account
- Payment Mode (Cash / Bank / Mobile Banking)
- Total Amount Paid
- Action Buttons (Edit, Print, Delete)

---

## Summary Section

The top section provides an overview such as:

- Total Supplier Payments
- Today’s Payments
- Monthly Payment Summary
- Total Outstanding Supplier Due

---

## Create Supplier Payment

This form is used to record a new supplier payment.

---

## Purpose

This form helps to:

- Pay supplier outstanding balances
- Record payment transactions
- Link payments with purchase invoices
- Maintain financial accuracy

---

## Supplier Payment Process

### Step 1: Select Supplier

Search and select the supplier (Company).

---

### Step 2: Enter Basic Information

Fill in:

- Current Date (auto or manual)
- Receipt No (Required)
- Payment Mode (Current Due Payment or similar)
- Payment Type (Cash, Bank, Mobile Banking)

---

### Step 3: Load Payment Lines

After selecting supplier, system loads:

- All outstanding purchase invoices
- Supplier due amount
- Invoice-wise breakdown

---

### Step 4: Enter Payment Amount

You can:

- Pay full invoice amount
- Pay partial amount
- Distribute payment across multiple invoices

---

### Step 5: Add Notes (Optional)

You may add:

- Payment reference
- Transaction notes
- Bank or mobile transaction ID

---

### Step 6: Save Payment

After saving:

- Supplier due is reduced
- Payment record is stored
- Invoice status is updated automatically

---

## Payment Methods

| Method         | Description             |
| -------------- | ----------------------- |
| Cash           | Physical cash payment   |
| Bank Transfer  | Direct bank transaction |
| Mobile Banking | bKash, Nagad, Rocket    |
| Cheque         | Cheque-based payment    |

---

## Business Rules

- Payments reduce supplier outstanding balance
- Partial payments are allowed
- Each payment must be linked to a supplier
- Payment history is permanent
- Invoice status updates automatically

---

## Payment Status Types

| Status  | Meaning                |
| ------- | ---------------------- |
| Paid    | Invoice fully cleared  |
| Partial | Partially paid         |
| Due     | Payment pending        |
| Advance | Extra payment recorded |

---

## Example Use Case

A supplier has a due balance of ৳20,000.

You make a payment:

- ৳10,000 via cash

System will:

- Reduce supplier due to ৳10,000
- Record payment history
- Update invoice status as partially paid

---

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake                  | Problem                           |
| ------------------------ | --------------------------------- |
| Wrong supplier selection | Payment applied to wrong account  |
| Not linking invoices     | Due balance not updated correctly |
| Incorrect amount entry   | Accounting mismatch               |
| Wrong payment mode       | Reporting errors                  |

---

## Best Practice

- Always verify supplier before payment
- Match payments with invoices when possible
- Keep receipt numbers consistent
- Avoid mixing payments between suppliers
- Use clear notes for tracking
