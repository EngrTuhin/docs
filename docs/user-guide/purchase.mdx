# Purchase & Procurement Operations

This guide explains how to manage your supply chain operations—from local suppliers and work orders to global import shipments (LC) and custom clearances.

---

## Who Can Access

* **Create Purchase Order (Maker)**: Purchase Officers, Inventory Staff.
* **Verify Purchase (Checker)**: Purchase Managers, Store Supervisors, Admins.
* **Work Orders & LC Module**: Procurement Directors, Operations Managers.
* **Suppliers & C&F Agent Directories**: Accounts, Procurement Admins.

---

## 1. Local Purchases & Maker-Checker Approvals

### Overview
Local purchases follow a two-tier control structure:
* **Purchase Entry (Maker)**: Logs purchase items, supplier details, warehouse, cost pricing, and deposits.
* **Purchase Record Verification (Checker)**: Approved by a supervisor to update inventory count.

### Navigation
* **Maker Entry**: Purchase Management → **Create Purchase Order**
* **Verification**: **Approval Inbox**

## 2. Supplier & C&F Agent Directories

### Overview
* **Suppliers Directory**: Registry of suppliers, opening balances, payment terms, and ledger history.
* **C&F Agent Directory**: Registry of clearing and forwarding agents who handle customs clearance for import LCs.

### Navigation
* **Suppliers**: Business Contacts → **Suppliers Directory**
* **C&F Directory**: Business Contacts → **C&F Directory**

### Step-by-Step Instructions (Create Supplier):
1. Navigate to Business Contacts → **Suppliers Directory** and click **Add Supplier**.
2. Enter the Supplier Name, Company Name, Email, Phone, and Address.
3. Enter their **Opening Balance** (outstanding due, if any, at system setup).
4. Click **Save Supplier**.

## 3. Work Orders & Work Order Merging

### Overview
* **Work Order List**: Track client specifications and requirements for custom production or bulk procurement.
* **Work Order Merger**: Combine multiple separate work orders into a single consolidated purchase order. This is helpful when importing bulk materials from a single vendor to reduce shipping overhead.

### Navigation
* **Work Orders**: Work Order Info → **Work Order List**
* **Work Order Merger**: Work Order Info → **Work Order Merger**

[Insert Screenshot Here - Work Order Merger Tool Interface]

### Step-by-Step Instructions (Merge Work Orders):
1. Go to Work Order Info → **Work Order Merger**.
2. Select the **Supplier** from whom you will purchase the merged items.
3. Select the active **Work Orders** you want to combine.
4. Click **Preview Merge** to review the consolidated items list.
5. Click **Confirm Merge & Create Purchase Order**. This compiles them into a single Purchase Order in the Maker queue.

## 4. LC Module (Import Costing)

### Overview
Manage international shipments. The Letter of Credit (LC) module tracks freight, customs duties, port charges, and C&F agent commissions, distributing them to compute the loaded **Landed Cost** of each item.

### Navigation
* **LC Module**: Purchase Management → **LC Module (Import Costing)**

### Field Descriptions (LC Details)

| Field | Description | Rules / Validations |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **LC Number** | Letter of credit ID from the bank. | Unique. |
| **Opening Bank** | Bank issuing the credit line. | Selected from Bank registry. |
| **Work Orders** | Linked work orders. | Must choose at least one work order. |
| **Import Expenses** | Port, custom, C&F agent fees. | Logged in the Expenses tab. |

### Step-by-Step Instructions (Configure LC Costing):
1. Go to Purchase Management → **LC Module** and click **Add LC**.
2. Fill in the LC Number, bank, open date, and choose the linked Work Orders.
3. In the **Expenses** tab, enter costs for customs clearance, insurance, transport, and select the C&F Agent.
4. The system calculates the Landed Cost Factor. Click **Save LC**.
5. Once the shipment arrives, click **Close LC & Receive Stock** to load the items into your warehouse at their loaded cost.

## Business Rules

* **Checker Restrictions**: A user who created a purchase order as a "Maker" is blocked from approving it as a "Checker" to prevent self-approval.
* **CPO to Work Order**: Customer Purchase Orders must be verified before they can be linked to a Work Order.
