# Business Contacts

Business Contacts is used to save people and company information related to your business.

You can add:

- Customers or retailers
- Suppliers
- Customer branches
- Sales persons
- C&F agents

<Info>
You do not need to set up everything. Add only the contacts your business needs.
</Info>

---

# Business Contacts Menu

You will find these options under **Business Contacts**.

| Menu | Use | Required? |
|---|---|---|
| Customers & Retailers Directory | Add customers or retailers | Usually required for sales |
| Suppliers Directory | Add suppliers | Usually required for purchase |
| Customer Branches | Add customer branch locations | Optional |
| Sales Persons Directory | Add sales team members | Optional |
| C&F Directory | Add clearing and forwarding agents | Optional |

---

# What Should You Set Up First?

For most businesses, start with these two:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add Customers or Retailers">
    Add the people, shops, or companies you sell products to.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Suppliers">
    Add the people or companies you purchase products from.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
Customer Branches, Sales Persons, and C&F Directory are optional. You can add them later if needed.
</Info>

---

# 1. Customers & Retailers Directory

Use this page to add customers or retailers.

Customers are used in:

- Sales Invoice
- Customer Due Collection
- Customer Advance Payment
- Customer Reports
- Customer Ledger

---

## Add Customer or Retailer

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Customers & Retailers Directory">
    From the left menu, go to **Business Contacts** and click **Customers & Retailers Directory**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Add">
    Click the green **Add** button.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter Name">
    Type the customer or retailer name.

    Example: `Rahim Store`
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter Contact Number">
    Type the customer phone number.

    Example: `017XXXXXXXX`
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Status">
    Select `Active` if this customer will be used in sales and reports.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select User Type">
    Select `Customer` or `Retailer`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Other Details If Needed">
    You may add proprietor, email, NID, invoice status, address, and contact person if needed.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save Product** to save the customer.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
In your system, the save button may show **Save Product** on different forms. Click that button to save the record.
</Warning>

---

## Customer Previous Due

If a customer already has due before starting this system, enter that amount as the customer opening balance if the customer form has an opening balance field.

Opening balance means the previous balance before using this system.

| Situation | What to Enter |
|---|---|
| Customer already owes you money | Enter the due amount |
| Customer has no previous due | Enter `0` or keep empty |

Example:

| Field | Example |
|---|---|
| Customer Name | `Rahim Store` |
| Opening Balance | `3000` |
| Opening Date | `2026-01-01` |

In this example, `3000` means **Rahim Store already owed 3,000 before you started using the system**.

<Info>
Customer opening balance helps customer due reports and customer ledger show the correct starting balance.
</Info>

<Warning>
Enter customer opening balance carefully. Wrong opening balance will make customer due and ledger reports incorrect.
</Warning>

# 2. Suppliers Directory

Use this page to add suppliers.

Suppliers are used in:

- Purchase Order
- Purchase Invoice
- Supplier Payment
- Purchase Return
- Supplier Reports
- Supplier Ledger

---

## Add Supplier

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Suppliers Directory">
    From the left menu, go to **Business Contacts** and click **Suppliers Directory**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Add">
    Click the green **Add** button.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter Supplier Name">
    Type the supplier name.

    Example: `ABC Pharma`
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter Contact Number">
    Type the supplier phone number.

    Example: `017XXXXXXXX`
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter Opening Balance">
    Enter the previous due amount if this supplier already has a balance before using this system.

    Example: If you already owe this supplier `5,000`, enter `5000`.

    If there is no previous due or advance balance, enter `0`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter Opening Date">
    Select the date from when this opening balance should start.

    Example: If you are starting the system from `2026-01-01`, use that date.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Status">
    Select `Active`.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Other Details If Needed">
    You may add proprietor, email, address, and brands if needed.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save Product** to save the supplier.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## What Is Opening Balance?

Opening Balance means the previous balance before starting to use this system.

Use Opening Balance when a supplier already has due or advance balance before you create new purchase records in the system.

| Situation | What to Enter |
|---|---|
| You already owe money to the supplier | Enter the due amount |
| Supplier already received advance from you | Enter the advance amount if your system supports advance balance |
| No previous due or advance | Enter `0` |

---

## What Is Opening Date?

Opening Date means the starting date of the opening balance.

Example:

If you start using the system on `2026-01-01`, and the supplier already has previous due of `5,000`, then enter:

| Field | Example |
|---|---|
| Opening Balance | `5000` |
| Opening Date | `2026-01-01` |

In this example, `5000` means **your business already owed 5,000 to this supplier before starting the system**.

<Info>
Opening Balance helps the supplier due report and supplier ledger show the correct balance from the beginning.
</Info>

<Warning>
Enter Opening Balance carefully. Wrong opening balance will make supplier due and ledger reports incorrect.
</Warning>

---

# 3. Customer Branches

Customer Branches are optional.

Use this only if one customer has multiple shops, offices, or delivery locations.

Example:

| Customer | Branch |
|---|---|
| Rahim Store | Mirpur Branch |
| Rahim Store | Uttara Branch |
| ABC Company | Dhaka Office |

---

## When to Use Customer Branches

Use Customer Branches when:

- A customer has more than one shop
- A customer has multiple office locations
- You need branch-wise delivery
- You need branch-wise sales tracking

---

## Add Customer Branch

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Customer Branches">
    From the left menu, go to **Business Contacts** and click **Customer Branches**.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Add">
    Click the green **Add** button.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Customer">
    Select the customer.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter Branch Name">
    Type the branch name.

    Example: `Mirpur Branch`
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Contact and Address If Needed">
    Add phone number, email, contact person, or address if needed.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save">
    Save the branch information.
  </Step>
</Steps>

# Quick Setup Guide

Use this simple guide:

| Business Need | What to Add |
|---|---|
| You sell products | Add Customers or Retailers |
| You purchase products | Add Suppliers |
| Customer has many branches | Add Customer Branches |
| You track sales by person | Add Sales Persons |
| You handle import or shipment | Add C&F Agents |
| Customer has previous due | Add customer opening balance if available |
| Supplier has previous due | Add supplier opening balance and opening date |

---

# Opening Balance Quick Example

Opening Balance is used only when there is previous due or previous balance.

| Contact Type | Situation | Example Entry |
|---|---|---|
| Customer | Customer already owes you money | Opening Balance `3000` |
| Supplier | You already owe supplier money | Opening Balance `5000` |
| No previous balance | Nothing is due | Opening Balance `0` |

<Info>
If there is no previous due, enter `0` or keep the field empty if the system allows it.
</Info>

---

# Best Practice

- Add only the contacts you need
- Keep phone numbers correct
- Avoid duplicate customer or supplier names
- Keep usable contacts `Active`
- Enter opening balance only when previous due exists
- Enter opening date when opening balance is used
- Add branches only when needed
- Add C&F only if your business uses it
- Add supplier brands if you want supplier-brand tracking

---

# Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Problem |
|---|---|
| Adding duplicate customers | Sales may go under the wrong customer |
| Wrong phone number | Communication becomes difficult |
| Wrong opening balance | Due report becomes incorrect |
| Missing opening date | Ledger starting date may be unclear |
| Wrong user type | Customer or retailer report may be wrong |
| Supplier not added before purchase | Purchase entry becomes difficult |
| Inactive contact | Contact may not appear in selection list |
